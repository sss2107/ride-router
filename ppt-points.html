Fastest realistic cab comparison on one iPhone

What can actually be parallelized

  • Not truly parallel app checks: iOS only lets one ride app be foregrounded at a time, so Grab, Gojek, CDG Zig, and TADA still have to be opened sequentially.
  • Parallel backend work: while iPhone Mirroring moves to the next app, the Mac OCRs already-captured screenshots in parallel and updates the cheapest fare as results come in.
  • Fixed Travel folder layout: the scanner skips slow OCR-based folder discovery and taps the known Travel-folder positions directly.

Why ads make OCR hard

  • CDG Zig and TADA can show promotional popups before the booking surface, hiding fare text entirely.
  • Ad copy often contains dollar amounts, promo percentages, vouchers, and reward text that look like fares.
  • The app now taps the known close area first, then filters obvious promo lines before picking the lowest fare-like price.

Speed direction

  • Launch apps from the fixed Travel folder coordinates.
  • Dismiss known ad surfaces immediately for CDG Zig and TADA.
  • Capture each screen quickly, then OCR in the background while the phone keeps moving.
  • Return partial results to the web app, so the cheapest option appears as soon as OCR finds it.
